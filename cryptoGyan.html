What is Blockchain?

It is the underlying technology used by Cryptocurrencies to make them safe, secure & “trustworthy

Blockchain enhances trust across a business network. It’s not that you can’t trust those whom you conduct business with; it’s that you don’t need to when operating on a blockchain network.

Blockchain uses a distributed ledger for keeping records of all the transactions in the network and is visible and accessible to all the participants of the network. Relevant consensus in the network decides the validity of a transaction and it is transparent & secure.

What is Cryptocurrency?

A ‘Cryptocurrency’ is a digital asset designed to work as a medium of exchange and a store of value, through a computer network that is not reliant on any central authority, such as a government or bank, to uphold or maintain it. Cryptocurrency does not exist in the physical form, like paper money and is not controlled by a centralized authority like a bank. Cryptocurrency is a tradable digital asset or digital form of money, built on blockchain technology.

Cryptocurrencies have been around since 2009 & their current high profile shows their popularity.

Traditional money, also known as fiat currencies, like INR, US Dollars or Euros, are government-issued. Cryptocurrencies don’t have an underlying backing from any central authority like a Bank or Government. Anyone who holds or owns blockchain currencies can save it, send it to other users or trade it within seconds via simple, digital transactions.

Your Checklist before investing

Here are some important pointers that you need to consider before investing into the Crypto World.

Research, Research, Do your own Research

Don’t invest into Crypto, just because your friends are buying or selling, do your own research, read whitepapers, educate yourself on things like the backing of the asset from the community, the supply limit of the coin, its use case etc. before taking any decision.

Understand Regulations

The Cryto world is evolving and so are the regulations. Keep yourself educated about the laws before venturing into the Cryto world. For example, In India, Crypto assets are unregulated. There may be no regulatory recourse for any loss from such transactions. Do conduct your own due diligence and consult your financial advisor before making any investment decisions.

Understanding the Risks of Trading

Cryptocurrencies are very volatile than your conventional assets, You need to fully understand the risks associated with trading in crypto assets. Invest if you fully understand the risks involve.

Stay Secure

Password Protection
Keep your passwords secure. It is your responsibility to keep them secure.
Multifactor Authentication
Always enable Multifactor authentication to add layers of security on your account and assets
Zero Trust on Cyber Attacks
Be aware of phishing and other cyber-attacks and keep yourself educated.
Coin Storage in Cold Wallets
Store your crypto assets in cold wallets if you are not actively trading.
